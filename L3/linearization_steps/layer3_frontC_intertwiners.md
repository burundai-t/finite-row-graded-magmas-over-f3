# Layer 3 Front C — closed form for \(\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}\)

**Version:** Layer 3 v1.3 / Front C.  
**Object:** the selected PAB magma \((M,\cdot)\), \(M=S\times S\), \(S=\mathbb F_3\).  
**Goal:** replace the verifier-only statement
\[
\dim \operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}=2
\]
by a closed symbolic formula.

Throughout, \(V=k[M]\) with \(k=\mathbb R\), and
\[
F_r=\operatorname{span}\{e_{(r,c)}:c\in S\}.
\]
For \(x\in M\), \(L_x\) denotes the left regular operator
\[
L_x e_y=e_{x\cdot y}.
\]
For an endomorphism \(\varphi\),
\[
\operatorname{Hom}_\varphi
=
\{T\in\operatorname{End}(V):T L_x=L_{\varphi(x)}T\ \forall x\in M\}.
\]
The constant endomorphism \(e_r\) is
\[
e_r(x)=(r,r)\qquad \forall x\in M.
\]

---

## 1. Final formula

Let
\[
\varepsilon:V\to k,
\qquad
\varepsilon\!\left(\sum_{m\in M}a_m e_m\right)=\sum_{m\in M}a_m
\]
be the total augmentation functional.

Define
\[
d_r=e_{(r,r)},
\qquad
 o_r=\sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}.
\]
Then
\[
\boxed{
\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}
=
\{u\varepsilon:u\in U_r\},
\qquad
U_r=\operatorname{span}\{d_r,o_r\}.
}
\]
Equivalently, every \(T\in\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}\) has the form
\[
\boxed{
T(v)=\varepsilon(v)\left(\alpha e_{(r,r)}+\beta\sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}\right)
}
\]
for unique \(\alpha,\beta\in k\).

Therefore
\[
\boxed{\dim\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}=2.}
\]

A useful orientation guardrail is:

\[
\boxed{
\text{the source functional is the universal augmentation }\varepsilon;
\text{ the two-dimensional freedom is in the target fixed space }U_r.
}
\]

---

## 2. Why \(\varepsilon\) appears

Each \(L_x\) is induced by a set map \(M\to M\), so every basis vector is sent to exactly one basis vector. Hence column sums are one and
\[
\boxed{\varepsilon L_x=\varepsilon\qquad \forall x\in M.}
\]

If \(u\in V\) satisfies
\[
L_{(r,r)}u=u,
\]
then \(T_u=u\varepsilon\) is an intertwiner for \(e_r\):
\[
T_uL_x=u\varepsilon L_x=u\varepsilon,
\]
while
\[
L_{(r,r)}T_u=(L_{(r,r)}u)\varepsilon=u\varepsilon.
\]
So
\[
\{u\varepsilon:u\in\operatorname{Fix}(L_{(r,r)})\}
\subseteq
\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}.
\]

---

## 3. Collapse lemma: every constant intertwiner factors through \(\varepsilon\)

Let
\[
\mathcal D=
\operatorname{span}\{(L_x-L_y)v:x,y\in M,\,v\in V\}.
\]
For \(T\in\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}\), the right side of the intertwining equation is independent of \(x\), because
\[
e_r(x)=(r,r)
\]
for all \(x\). Therefore
\[
T L_x=L_{(r,r)}T=T L_y
\]
for all \(x,y\), and hence
\[
T\mathcal D=0.
\]

Since \(\varepsilon L_x=\varepsilon\), every generator of \(\mathcal D\) has augmentation zero. Thus
\[
\mathcal D\subseteq\ker\varepsilon.
\]

The reverse inclusion is finite and structural: the graph on \(M\) generated by relations
\[
x\cdot z\sim y\cdot z
\]
with common source \(z\) is connected. The verifier writes an explicit spanning tree in

```text
tables/layer3_frontC_collapse_tree.csv
```

For example,
\[
e_{(0,2)}-e_{(0,0)}=(L_{(0,1)}-L_{(0,0)})e_{(0,0)},
\]
\[
e_{(1,0)}-e_{(0,0)}=(L_{(1,0)}-L_{(0,0)})e_{(0,0)},
\]
and the full eight-edge tree spans all augmentation-zero differences. Hence
\[
\boxed{\mathcal D=\ker\varepsilon.}
\]

Therefore every \(T\in\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}\) kills \(\ker\varepsilon\), so it factors uniquely through the one-dimensional quotient
\[
V/\ker\varepsilon\cong k.
\]
Thus
\[
T=u\varepsilon
\]
for a unique vector \(u\in V\).

---

## 4. The fixed space of \(L_{(r,r)}\)

Substituting \(T=u\varepsilon\) into the intertwining equation gives
\[
u\varepsilon=L_{(r,r)}u\,\varepsilon.
\]
Since \(\varepsilon\ne0\), this is equivalent to
\[
L_{(r,r)}u=u.
\]
So
\[
\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}
\cong
\operatorname{Fix}(L_{(r,r)}).
\]

Now \(L_{(r,r)}\) has image in \(F_r\). Therefore a fixed vector must lie in \(F_r\). On \(F_r\), the operator fixes \(e_{(r,r)}\) and swaps the two off-diagonal basis vectors:
\[
L_{(r,r)}e_{(r,r)}=e_{(r,r)},
\]
\[
L_{(r,r)}e_{(r,c)}=e_{(r,\overline{rc})}
\qquad(c\ne r).
\]
The fixed space is therefore exactly
\[
\boxed{
\operatorname{Fix}(L_{(r,r)})
=
\operatorname{span}\left\{
 e_{(r,r)},
 \sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}
\right\}.
}
\]
This proves the final formula.

---

## 5. Basis operators

For each \(r\), a convenient basis of \(\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}\) is:
\[
D_r=e_{(r,r)}\varepsilon,
\]
\[
O_r=\left(\sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}\right)\varepsilon.
\]
In matrix language, every column of \(D_r\) is \(e_{(r,r)}\), and every column of \(O_r\) is \(\sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}\). Each nonzero element has rank one unless \(u=0\).

Explicitly:

| \(r\) | basis vector 1 | basis vector 2 |
|---:|---|---|
| 0 | \(e_{(0,0)}\varepsilon\) | \((e_{(0,1)}+e_{(0,2)})\varepsilon\) |
| 1 | \(e_{(1,1)}\varepsilon\) | \((e_{(1,0)}+e_{(1,2)})\varepsilon\) |
| 2 | \(e_{(2,2)}\varepsilon\) | \((e_{(2,0)}+e_{(2,1)})\varepsilon\) |

## 6. Automorphism intertwiners retained

For automorphisms
\[
\varphi_\rho(r,c)=(\rho(r),\rho(c)),
\qquad \rho\in S_3,
\]
the known formula remains:
\[
\boxed{\operatorname{Hom}_{\varphi_\rho}=kP_\rho.}
\]
Only for \(\rho=\mathrm{id}\) is this \(kI\). For nontrivial \(\rho\), the generator is the corresponding permutation matrix.

The verifier checks the constraint rank:
\[
\dim\operatorname{Hom}_{\varphi_\rho}=1,
\qquad
\dim\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}=2.
\]
Thus
\[
\boxed{
\sum_{\varphi\in\operatorname{End}(M,\cdot)}
\dim\operatorname{Hom}_\varphi
=6\cdot1+3\cdot2=12.
}
\]

## 8. Final Front C theorem

\[
\boxed{
\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}
=
\left\{
\left(\alpha e_{(r,r)}+\beta\sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}\right)\varepsilon
:
\alpha,\beta\in k
\right\}.
}
\]

Consequently,
\[
\boxed{
\dim\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}=2
}
\]
for each \(r\in S\), and the total intertwiner dimension over the endomorphism monoid is
\[
\boxed{6\cdot1+3\cdot2=12.}
\]

Thus Front L3-C is closed: the constant-endomorphism intertwiners are no longer described by a numerical nullspace, but by a short structural factorization
\[
\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}
\cong
\operatorname{Fix}(L_{(r,r)}),
\qquad
T=u\varepsilon.
\]
