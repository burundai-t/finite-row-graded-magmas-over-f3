# Layer 3 Front D — PAB identities and finite binary presentation

**Version:** Layer 3 v1.5 / Front D.  
**Status:** closed at the binary term-function level; compact all-arity identity basis not established.

Front D starts from the identity block of Layer 3:

\[
I_1:\quad (xy)(xy)=xx,
\]

\[
I_2:\quad (xx)(yy)=(xy)(yx),
\]

\[
I_3:\quad (xx)(yx)=(xy)(yy),
\]

\[
(\star):\quad z(zw)=(zw)z.
\]

The old Layer 3 text already recorded these identities as true and left two related questions open: whether they form a finite basis and what the exact free PAB-magma behaviour is. Front D resolves the binary term-function part and adds countermodels showing that the compact identity package is not complete.

---

## 1. Classical identities are true but incomplete

The verifier checks directly in PAB:

\[
I_1,I_2,I_3,(\star)\quad\text{all hold.}
\]

However, they do **not** generate all PAB identities.

A PAB identity already visible in the one-variable power cycle is

\[
U_1:\quad x(x(xx))=xx.
\]

There is a 3-element countermodel satisfying

\[
I_1,I_2,I_3,(\star)
\]

but violating \(U_1\). Its multiplication table, in row-major order on \(\{0,1,2\}\), is

\[
(0,0,1,\;0,0,1,\;1,1,0).
\]

The witness is \(x=2\):

\[
x(x(xx))=1,
\qquad
xx=0.
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{I_1,I_2,I_3,(\star)\text{ are not a complete PAB identity basis.}}
\]

---

## 2. Unary fiber-cycle identities

Let

\[
s=x^2=xx,
\qquad
u=x(xx).
\]

Inside the row-fiber generated by \(x\), PAB has a three-point Steiner cycle. Besides the star instance

\[
x(xx)=(xx)x,
\]

Front D records two useful unary consequences:

\[
U_1:\quad x(x(xx))=xx,
\]

\[
U_2:\quad (xx)(x(xx))=x.
\]

Equivalently, if \(s=x^2\) and \(\nu=xs\), then

\[
x\nu=s,
\qquad
s\nu=x.
\]

These identities are true in PAB and explain why one-variable chains are periodic.

But even the enriched compact package

\[
I_1,I_2,I_3,(\star),U_1,U_2
\]

is still not complete. A second 3-element countermodel satisfies all six identities above but violates the PAB identity

\[
D_5:\quad x(yy)=x((y(xy))x).
\]

The countermodel table is

\[
(0,1,2,\;1,0,1,\;2,1,0).
\]

With \(x=2,y=1\), the two sides evaluate to

\[
2\ne0.
\]

Thus Front D does not overclaim a small human-readable all-arity basis.

---

## 3. Complete binary term-function algebra

Let \(T_2(\mathrm{PAB})\) be the algebra of binary term functions

\[
M^2\to M
\]

generated by the two projections \(x,y\) under pointwise PAB multiplication.

Since \(M\) is finite, \(T_2(\mathrm{PAB})\subseteq M^{M^2}\) is finite. Front D computes the exact generated subalgebra:

\[
\boxed{|T_2(\mathrm{PAB})|=630.}
\]

## 4. Finite complete presentation for binary identities

Choose canonical representatives

\[
R_0,R_1,\ldots,R_{629}
\]

for the 630 binary term functions. The package includes:

```text
tables/layer3_frontD_canonical_representatives.csv
tables/layer3_frontD_binary_clone_multiplication_table.csv
```

The multiplication table defines

\[
\mu(i,j)=k
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
R_iR_j\equiv R_k
\]

as binary PAB term functions.

Thus the finite presentation

\[
\boxed{
R_iR_j=R_{\mu(i,j)}
\qquad
0\le i,j<630
}
\]

is complete for two-variable PAB term functions: every binary term reduces recursively to exactly one of the 630 states, and two binary terms are PAB-identical iff they reduce to the same state.

This is a finite presentation of the **binary term-function algebra**, not a compact equational basis for all arities.

## 5. Status after Front D

Closed:

\[
\boxed{|T_2(\mathrm{PAB})|=630.}
\]

\[
\boxed{\text{binary term-function stabilization occurs at size }15.}
\]

\[
\boxed{I_1,I_2,I_3,(\star)\text{ are true but incomplete.}}
\]

\[
\boxed{I_1,I_2,I_3,(\star),U_1,U_2\text{ are still incomplete.}}
\]

\[
\boxed{\text{the 630-state table gives a complete finite binary presentation.}}
\]

Still open / not claimed:

\[
\boxed{\text{a small human-readable all-arity finite basis of PAB identities.}}
\]

\[
\boxed{\text{a proof that no such compact basis exists.}}
\]

Front E should therefore be reframed. The two-generated binary term-function algebra is finite, so the old “infinite or stabilization?” question is answered for binary functions. Remaining free-algebra work should focus on either arity growth, a compact all-arity equational basis, or a structural compression of the 630-state presentation.
