## 3. Magma algebra \(k[M]\)

Let \(k=\mathbb R\) unless otherwise stated. Define
\[
k[M]=\bigoplus_{x\in M} k\,e_x.
\]

The magma product extends bilinearly:
\[
\left(\sum_x a_xe_x\right)\cdot
\left(\sum_y b_ye_y\right)

\sum_{x,y}a_xb_y\,e_{x\cdot y}.

As a vector space,
\[
k[M]\cong \mathrm{Mat}_3(k),

This is not an algebra isomorphism. The PAB product and the usual matrix product are two different bilinear operations on the same \(9\)-dimensional carrier.

\]

The matrix-product structure is a companion structure discussed in §11, not the PAB multiplication itself.

## 5. Spectrum, minimal polynomials, and Jordan structure — Front A closed

Layer 3 v1 recorded the spectral split by verifier. Layer 3 v1.1 closes Front L3-A by deriving it from the row-fiber normal form of each left translation. The verifier now audits the proof normal forms rather than serving as the only source of the statement.

### 5.1. Row-fiber normal form

Let
\[
V=k[M],\qquad F_q=\operatorname{span}\{e_{(q,d)}:d\in S\}.
\]
For fixed \(x=(r,c)\), write
\[
L_x=\iota_r A_x,
\]
where \(A_x:V\to F_r\) is \(L_x\) with codomain restricted to the target row, and \(\iota_r:F_r\hookrightarrow V\) is inclusion. Define the fiber block
\[
B_{r,c}=A_x\iota_r=L_x|_{F_r}:F_r\to F_r.
\]

For a source row \(q\ne r\), PAB gives
\[
A_x(e_{(q,d)})=e_{(r,q)}\qquad(d\in S).
\]
Thus each cross-row source fiber is collapsed by the augmentation functional onto the target basis vector indexed by the source row.

On the same row,
\[
B_{r,c}e_{(r,d)}=
\begin{cases}
e_{(r,r)},& d=c,\\
e_{(r,\overline{cd})},& d\ne c.
\end{cases}
\]

Because \(A_x\iota_r=B_{r,c}\), the powers satisfy
\[
\boxed{L_x^n=\iota_r B_{r,c}^{\,n-1}A_x\qquad(n\ge1).}
\]
The block form is therefore
\[
L_x=\begin{pmatrix}
B_{r,c}&U_s&U_t\\
0&0&0\\
0&0&0
\end{pmatrix}
\]
for \(\{s,t\}=S\setminus\{r\}\). Hence
\[
\boxed{\chi_{L_x}(\lambda)=\lambda^6\chi_{B_{r,c}}(\lambda).}
\]

Finally, \(A_x\) is onto \(F_r\): the two cross-row blocks give \(e_{(r,s)}\) and \(e_{(r,t)}\), while \(B_{r,c}e_{(r,c)}=e_{(r,r)}\). Therefore
\[
\boxed{\operatorname{rank}L_x=3,
\qquad
\dim\ker L_x=6.}
\]

---

### 5.2. Diagonal case

Let \(x=(r,r)\), and let \(s,t\) be the two elements of \(S\setminus\{r\}\). Then
\[
B_{r,r}e_{(r,r)}=e_{(r,r)},
\qquad
B_{r,r}e_{(r,s)}=e_{(r,t)},
\qquad
B_{r,r}e_{(r,t)}=e_{(r,s)}.
\]
So
\[
B_{r,r}^2=I_{F_r},
\qquad
\chi_{B_{r,r}}(\lambda)=(\lambda-1)^2(\lambda+1).
\]
Using \(L_x^n=\iota_rB^{n-1}A_x\),
\[
L_x^3=\iota_rB_{r,r}^{2}A_x=\iota_rA_x=L_x.
\]
Thus \(L_x\) is annihilated by
\[
\lambda(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1).
\]
The eigenvalues \(1\), \(-1\), and \(0\) all occur: \(1\) and \(-1\) already occur on \(F_r\), and \(0\) occurs because \(\operatorname{rank}L_x=3<9\). Therefore the minimal polynomial is exactly
\[
\boxed{\mu_{L_x}(\lambda)=\lambda(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1).}
\]
The characteristic polynomial is
\[
\boxed{\chi_{L_x}(\lambda)=\lambda^6(\lambda-1)^2(\lambda+1).}
\]
The zero algebraic multiplicity is \(6\), and \(\dim\ker L_x=6\). Since the minimal polynomial is square-free, \(L_x\) is semisimple.

---

### 5.3. Off-diagonal case

Let \(x=(r,c)\) with \(c\ne r\), and put
\[
u=\overline{rc}=-r-c\pmod3.
\]
Then \(\{r,c,u\}=S\), and the same-row block is
\[
B_{r,c}e_{(r,c)}=e_{(r,r)},
\qquad
B_{r,c}e_{(r,r)}=e_{(r,u)},
\qquad
B_{r,c}e_{(r,u)}=e_{(r,r)}.
\]
The vectors
\[
e_{(r,r)}+e_{(r,u)},
\qquad
e_{(r,r)}-e_{(r,u)},
\qquad
e_{(r,c)}-e_{(r,u)}
\]
are eigenvectors of \(B_{r,c}\) with eigenvalues \(1\), \(-1\), and \(0\), respectively. Hence
\[
\chi_{B_{r,c}}(\lambda)=\lambda(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1),
\qquad
B_{r,c}^3=B_{r,c},
\qquad
\operatorname{rank}B_{r,c}=2.
\]
Consequently,
\[
\boxed{\chi_{L_x}(\lambda)=\lambda^7(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1).}
\]
The power identity becomes
\[
L_x^4=\iota_rB_{r,c}^{3}A_x=\iota_rB_{r,c}A_x=L_x^2.
\]
So \(L_x\) is annihilated by
\[
\lambda^2(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1).
\]

The square on the zero factor is necessary. Choose any basis vector \(y\in F_c\). Since \(c\ne r\), this is a cross-row input, and
\[
L_x y=e_{(r,c)}.
\]
But
\[
L_x^3y=
\iota_rB_{r,c}^{2}A_xy
=\iota_rB_{r,c}^{2}e_{(r,c)}
=e_{(r,u)}\ne e_{(r,c)}.
\]
Therefore
\[
L_x^3\ne L_x.
\]
The polynomial \(\lambda(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1)\) does not annihilate \(L_x\), while \(\lambda^2(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1)\) does. Since the \(\pm1\) eigenspaces occur already inside \(F_r\), the exact minimal polynomial is
\[
\boxed{\mu_{L_x}(\lambda)=\lambda^2(\lambda-1)(\lambda+1).}
\]

The algebraic multiplicity of \(0\) is \(7\), from \(\chi_{L_x}\). The geometric multiplicity is
\[
\dim\ker L_x=9-\operatorname{rank}L_x=6.
\]
Moreover
\[
\operatorname{rank}L_x^2=\operatorname{rank}B_{r,c}=2,
\qquad
\dim\ker L_x^2=7.
\]
Thus the zero-primary Jordan structure has six blocks with total size seven and nilpotent index two. Therefore
\[
\boxed{
J_0(L_x)=J_2(0)\oplus J_1(0)^{\oplus5}.
}
\]
This proves the central Front A distinction:
\[
\boxed{
\text{diagonal }L_x\text{ are semisimple, while off-diagonal }L_x\text{ carry exactly one }J_2(0).
}
\]

## 6. Composition envelope — Front B closed

Let
\[
\operatorname{Mon}^+\langle L_x\rangle
\]
denote the **positive** composition monoid/semigroup generated by the nine left translations \(L_x\), without adjoining an external identity map.

Front B replaces the earlier closure computation by a structural normal-form theorem.

### 6.1. Same-row block semigroup

For \(x=(r,a)\), the restriction of \(L_x\) to the source fiber \(F_r\) is the map
\[
B_{r,a}:S\to S,
\]
\[
B_{r,a}(c)=
\begin{cases}
r, & c=a,\\
\overline{ac}, & c\ne a.
\end{cases}
\]

Let
\[
\mathcal B_r=\langle B_{r,0},B_{r,1},B_{r,2}\rangle
\]
inside the full transformation semigroup of \(S\).

Define \(\tau_r\) as the transposition fixing \(r\) and swapping the two elements of \(S\setminus\{r\}\). Also define
\[
N^{(r)}_{p,q}(r)=p,\qquad
N^{(r)}_{p,q}(c)=q\quad(c\ne r).
\]

Then
\[
\boxed{
\mathcal B_r
=
\{\operatorname{id},\tau_r\}
\cup
\{N^{(r)}_{p,q}:p\ne q,\ p=r\text{ or }q=r\}.
}
\]

Hence
\[
\boxed{|\mathcal B_r|=6.}
\]

For example, for \(r=0\):
\[
\mathcal B_0=\{011,012,021,022,100,200\},
\]
where \(012=\operatorname{id}\), \(021=\tau_0\), and the remaining four are rank-two maps constant on \(S\setminus\{0\}\).

### 6.2. Positive composition normal forms

Let \(K_{r,t}\) be the global constant map
\[
K_{r,t}(s,c)=(r,t).
\]

For \(U\in\mathcal B_r\) and \(a\in S\), define
\[
T_{r,U,a}(s,c)=
\begin{cases}
(r,U(B_{r,a}(c))), & s=r,\\
(r,U(s)), & s\ne r.
\end{cases}
\]

Then every positive composition word in the \(L_x\)'s is exactly one of:
\[
\boxed{K_{r,t}}
\]
or
\[
\boxed{T_{r,U,a}}.
\]

The induction is short. Initially,
\[
L_{r,a}=T_{r,\operatorname{id},a}.
\]
If a new left factor \(L_{r,a}\) changes target row from \(s\) to \(r\), the previous word has image in \(F_s\), so
\[
L_{r,a}\circ T=K_{r,s}.
\]
If the target row stays \(r\), then
\[
L_{r,a}\circ T_{r,U,b}=T_{r,B_{r,a}\circ U,b},
\]
and
\[
L_{r,a}\circ K_{r,t}=K_{r,B_{r,a}(t)}.
\]
Because \(\mathcal B_r\) is closed, the normal forms are closed.

### 6.3. Count of positive monoid elements

The constants contribute three maps:
\[
K_{r,0},K_{r,1},K_{r,2}.
\]

For row-stay maps \(T_{r,U,a}\):

- the two bijective \(U\)'s, \(\operatorname{id}\) and \(\tau_r\), give \(2\cdot3=6\) distinct maps;
- the four rank-two \(U=N^{(r)}_{p,q}\) maps are constant on \(S\setminus\{r\}\), so \(a\ne r\) gives a single duplicated class; each such \(U\) contributes \(2\) distinct maps.

Therefore
\[
\#\{T_{r,U,a}\}_{distinct}=2\cdot3+4\cdot2=14.
\]

Adding constants:
\[
\boxed{
|W_r\cap\operatorname{Mon}^+\langle L_x\rangle|
=
14+3=17.
}
\]

Since target rows are disjoint,
\[
\boxed{
|\operatorname{Mon}^+\langle L_x\rangle|
=
3\cdot17
=
51.
}
\]

## 7. Off-diagonal directed-edge representation

Let
\[
M^\times=\{(r,c)\in M:r\ne c\}.
\]

This is the set of six directed edges on a \(3\)-point set.

The two canonical maps are:
\[
C(r,c)=(c,\overline{rc}),
\qquad
J(r,c)=(c,r).
\]

They satisfy:
\[
C^3=1,\qquad J^2=1,\qquad JCJ=C^{-1}.
\]

Thus
\[
\langle C,J\rangle\cong S_3\cong D_3.
\]

### 7.1. Corrected \(S_3\)-character

The \(S_3\)-action on \(\mathbb R[M^\times]\) has character:
\[
\boxed{\chi=(6,0,0)}
\]
on the conjugacy classes
\[
(\mathrm{id},\ 3\text{-cycle},\ \text{transposition}).
\]

The transposition fixes no directed off-diagonal edge: if a transposition fixes one vertex, the only pair fixed coordinatewise would be \((v,v)\), which is not in \(M^\times\).

Therefore:
\[
\mathbb R[M^\times]\cong
\mathbf1\oplus\mathrm{sign}\oplus2\,\mathrm{std}.
\]

The old count \((6,0,2)\) is not retained in v1.

### 7.2. Absorption matrix

Define absorption:
\[
x\triangleright y
\iff
x\cdot y=x,
\qquad x,y\in M^\times.
\]

For PAB:
\[
\operatorname{Abs}(x)=\{C(x),J(x)\}.
\]

Let \(A_{\mathrm{abs}}\) be the \(6\times6\) matrix
\[
(A_{\mathrm{abs}})_{x,y}=[x\cdot y=x].
\]

With permutation matrices written as \(P_f e_x=e_{f(x)}\):
\[
\boxed{A_{\mathrm{abs}}=(P_C+P_J)^\top.}
\]

The spectrum is:
\[
\boxed{\operatorname{Spec}(A_{\mathrm{abs}})=\{2,0,0,0,-1,-1\}.}
\]

Interpretation:
- \(\lambda=2\) is the uniform outdegree \(2\);
- the two \(-1\) modes come from the standard component;
- the zero modes encode the degeneracy of the two-transition absorption graph.

### 8.1. Endomorphism monoid

The PAB endomorphism monoid has \(9\) elements:

for \(\rho\in S_3\), and
\[

### 8.2. Intertwiner spaces

For an endomorphism \(\varphi\), define
\[
\operatorname{Hom}_\varphi
=
\{T\in\operatorname{End}(k[M]):
T L_x=L_{\varphi(x)}T\ \forall x\in M\}.
\]

For automorphisms:
\[
\boxed{\operatorname{Hom}_{\varphi_\rho}=\mathbb R\,P_\rho,\qquad \dim=1.}
\]

Only for \(\rho=\mathrm{id}\) is this \(\mathbb R I\). For nontrivial \(\rho\), the generator is the corresponding permutation matrix.

For constants, Front C now gives the closed formula. Let
\[
\varepsilon: k[M]\to k,
\qquad
\varepsilon\!\left(\sum_{m\in M}a_m e_m\right)=\sum_{m\in M}a_m
\]
be the total augmentation. Put
\[
d_r=e_{(r,r)},
\qquad
o_r=\sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}.
\]
Then
\[
\boxed{
\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}
=
\{u\varepsilon:u\in U_r\},
\qquad
U_r=\operatorname{span}\{d_r,o_r\}.
}
\]
Equivalently, every constant-endomorphism intertwiner has the unique form
\[
\boxed{
T(v)=\varepsilon(v)
\left(
\alpha e_{(r,r)}+\beta\sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}
\right),
\qquad \alpha,\beta\in k.
}
\]
Hence
\[
\boxed{\dim\operatorname{Hom}_{e_r}=2.}
\]

The proof is the factorization
\[
T(L_x-L_y)=0
\quad\forall x,y
\Rightarrow
T\text{ kills }\ker\varepsilon
\Rightarrow
T=u\varepsilon,
\]
using the finite collapse lemma that the differences of left translations generate \(\ker\varepsilon\). The remaining condition is
\[
L_{(r,r)}u=u,
\]
and
\[
\operatorname{Fix}(L_{(r,r)})
=
\operatorname{span}
\left\{
 e_{(r,r)},
 \sum_{c\ne r}e_{(r,c)}
\right\}.
\]

Total:
\[
\boxed{
\sum_{\varphi\in\operatorname{End}(M,\cdot)}
\dim\operatorname{Hom}_\varphi
=
6\cdot1+3\cdot2=12.
}
\]

This is now a structural linear passport for the endomorphism structure, not only a nullspace count.
